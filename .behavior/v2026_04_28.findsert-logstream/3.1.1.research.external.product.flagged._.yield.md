# research: cloudwatch logs cost

the vision flagged one topic for research: "cloudwatch logs cost? | [research] | verify in research phase"

---

## summary

cloudwatch logs cost is reasonable for most usecases. the primary cost driver is **ingestion** at $0.50/GB in standard regions. for typical service logs (10-100 MB/day), cost is negligible. for high-volume scenarios, tiered pricing and infrequent access log class reduce costs significantly.

---

## findings

### ingestion cost

- [FACT] standard ingestion cost is **$0.50 per GB** in US East (N. Virginia) [1][2][3][4]
- [FACT] lambda logs have **tiered pricing** since May 2025, as low as $0.05/GB at highest volume tier [6][8]
- [FACT] infrequent access log class reduces ingestion by **50%** (to ~$0.25/GB) [5][9]

### storage cost

- [FACT] storage cost is **$0.03 per GB per month** after free tier [1][4]
- [FACT] retention policy controls storage duration — shorter = cheaper [7][9]

### query cost

- [FACT] cloudwatch logs insights charges **$0.005 per GB scanned** [1][4]

### free tier

- [FACT] free tier includes **5 GB of log data ingestion** per month [1][2]
- [FACT] free tier includes **5 GB of log data storage** [1]

### rate limits

- [FACT] PutLogEvents limit is **5,000 TPS per account per region** [3][10]
- [FACT] per-stream limit is **5 requests per second** [10]
- [FACT] batch limit is **1 MB per batch, 10,000 events per batch** [3][10]

### cost estimates for typical usecases

| usecase | daily volume | monthly cost (ingestion only) |
|---------|--------------|-------------------------------|
| cli tool (local dev) | 1-10 MB | < $0.01 |
| ec2 service (moderate) | 100 MB | ~$1.50 |
| ec2 service (high volume) | 1 GB | ~$15 |
| high-scale service | 10 GB | ~$150 |

- [SUMP] most usecases for this library (cli tools, ec2 services) will fall in the "negligible cost" range

### cost optimization options

- [FACT] use infrequent access log class for 50% savings [5][9]
- [FACT] set retention policies to limit storage [7][9]
- [FACT] batch logs to reduce API calls [3][10]
- [OPIN] for most usecases targeted by this library, default settings are cost-effective — optimization is premature [5]
